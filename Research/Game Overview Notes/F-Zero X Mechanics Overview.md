### Core Racing and Energy System

- **High‑speed hover racing**:  
  Machines glide just above the track and can reach extreme speeds (well over 1,000 km/h), so small steering inputs and early corrections matter more than heavy braking. The game is tuned around always‑on intensity, with 30 machines sharing the track at once.

- **Energy as health and boost**:  
  Your energy bar serves two purposes:
  - **Health** – If it reaches zero, your machine explodes and you’re out.
  - **Boost fuel** – From lap 2 onward in Grand Prix (or from the start in some modes), you can trade energy for a powerful **boost**.  
  This creates a constant risk‑reward loop: boosting aggressively improves lap times and overtakes, but leaves you fragile to collisions and falls.

- **Energy recovery**:  
  - **Pit zones** on the track slowly refill your energy if you pass through them.  
  - In some modes, **retiring opponents** (destroying rival machines) can also reward extra energy or lives, encouraging aggressive play.

### Attacks and Close‑Quarters Combat

- **Side Attack**:
  - Performed by quickly double‑tapping the left or right shoulder (Z or R on N64) in that direction.
  - Your machine lunges sideways with a red outline, hitting hard and often pushing rivals into guard rails or off the track.
  - Skilled players also use side attacks on curves to maintain speed while adjusting their line.

- **Spin Attack**:
  - Triggered by rapidly rotating the stick while pressing attack (or equivalent input), causing your machine to spin.
  - Damages nearby opponents in all directions, acting as area‑control in dense packs.
  - Useful when surrounded or when you want to clear space without committing to a specific direction.

Because attacks deal real damage and can knock rivals off the course entirely, **positioning**—where you are relative to machines and guard rails—is as important as raw pace.

### Track Features and Handling

- **Three‑dimensional track design**:
  - Tracks use **pipes, tunnels, half‑loops, corkscrews, and massive jumps**.
  - Many sections have open edges; falling off means instant retirement, so lines and camera awareness are critical.

- **Turning techniques**:
  - **Standard steering** for gentle bends.
  - **Drift/slide turns** by combining steering with shoulder inputs for tighter corners.
  - The fastest players blend steering, sliding, and side attacks to hug guard rails without crashing.

- **Start boost (Rocket Start)**:
  - With precise throttle timing at the countdown, you can gain an early launch advantage off the starting line.

### Modes and Progression

- **Grand Prix**:  
  Multi‑race cups (Jack, Queen, King, plus unlockable Joker and X Cup) with points based on finishing position and bonuses for retiring opponents. Surviving all 30 machines and placing well across tracks is the main challenge.

- **Time Attack / Practice**:  
  Focus on learning lines and optimizing boosts without the chaos of 29 opponents.

- **Death Race**:  
  A continuous track where the sole objective is to **retire all other machines as quickly as possible**, emphasizing combat and efficient use of attacks over finishing position.

Across all modes, F‑Zero X’s mechanics emphasize **raw speed, track mastery, and aggressive contact racing**. Mastery comes from learning when to spend or save energy, how to use side and spin attacks without losing control, and how to hold perfect lines on tracks that are constantly trying to throw you into space.
