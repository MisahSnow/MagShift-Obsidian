### Core Wipeout Mechanics

- **Anti‑gravity racing**:  
  Ships hover slightly above the track. They don’t steer like cars; they “slide” and pivot, so the game is about **smooth lines** and **managing drift** rather than braking to a stop for corners.

- **Steering + air‑brakes**:  
  - **Steering**: Standard left/right input that turns the ship.  
  - **Air‑brakes**: Separate **left and right brake triggers**:
    - Pressing the **left air‑brake** makes the ship pivot hard to the left.
    - Pressing the **right air‑brake** pivots hard to the right.
    - Tapping them lets you “feather” into corners; holding them gives a sharp, drifting turn but costs speed.  
  Good play is usually **steering + little air‑brake taps** instead of fully slamming them.

### Track Features

- **Speed pads**:
  - Glowing strips on the track (often blue or orange depending on the game).
  - Driving over one gives a **short burst of extra speed**.
  - The fastest laps come from **chaining speed pads** while keeping a clean racing line and avoiding walls.

- **Weapon pads**:
  - Cross‑shaped pads that give you a **weapon or powerup** when you drive over them.
  - You can normally hold **only one pickup at a time**, so you choose:
    - Use it to attack.
    - Use it to defend or boost.
    - In some games, **absorb** it to restore shield/energy.

- **Mag‑strips (magnetic sections)**:
  - Parts of the track where the ship is **magnetically locked** to the surface.
  - Let designers do **extreme banking, corkscrews, or upside‑down sections** without you flying off.
  - Handling feels a bit heavier/locked‑in, emphasizing **line choice** more than airtime tricks.

- **Jumps and elevation changes**:
  - Ramps and crests launch you briefly into the air.
  - In some entries you can use airtime for extra tech (like barrel rolls), trading **shield/health for extra speed**.
  - Bad timing can make you overshoot corners or land into walls, so jumps are both an opportunity and a risk.

### Ship Stats (Why They Feel Different)

- **Top speed** – How fast the ship can go on straights.  
- **Handling** – How easily it turns and how forgiving it is in tight bends.  
- **Thrust / acceleration** – How quickly it gets back to speed after braking or getting hit.  
- **Shield / armor** – How much damage it can take from weapons and walls.  

These stats, plus how you use **air‑brakes, speed pads, and weapon pads**, define the game’s feel: learning tracks and timing your inputs turns chaos into a controllable, high‑speed flow.
