### Core Feel: Anti‑Gravity + Precision Control

Redout games are built around **very high-speed anti‑gravity racing** where the difference between fast and crashed is usually a matter of micro-corrections. Instead of “braking to turn,” you keep momentum while shaping your trajectory.

Two movement layers are especially important:

- **Strafing (side movement)**: helps you line up for corner entry and make small positional corrections without immediately losing speed.
- **Tilt / pitch control**: lets you change the craft’s attitude so it stays stable on curved sections, over jumps, and through fast transitions.

The best drivers manage these continuously, so the vehicle feels “glued” to the optimal racing path rather than pushed around by the track.

### Boost, Energy, and Risk–Reward

Boost is the main engine of performance in Redout.

- In **Redout 2**, boost and energy are tied to your ship’s survivability: using boost aggressively can pay off in lap time, but overusing it can damage your craft or leave you vulnerable if you hit something.
- In both games, there’s a strong incentive to learn when boosting helps most (straight/entry points) versus when it increases your crash risk (tight apexes, dense traffic, low-visibility sections).

This creates a constant tension: commit to speed now, or preserve control and stability for later.

### Track Design and Mastery

Redout tracks are built to be read at speed. Players typically improve by:

- learning how different track shapes “flow” together (entry → apex → exit),
- understanding where you can stay stable at maximum speed,
- and adjusting strafing/tilt patterns to avoid scraping walls or losing traction on fast transitions.

As with WipEout and F‑Zero, mastery is where Redout becomes satisfying: once your inputs feel consistent, the game turns into repeatable, improvable performance.

### Combat / Powerups (Game-Dependent)

Redout is usually less weapon-heavy than classic arcade shooters, but it still supports the WipEout-style fantasy of racing while managing extra tools:

- **Redout 1** includes a system where you can use purchasable power-ups (with active/passive options).
- **Redout 2** emphasizes customization and boost/handling mastery more than constant weapon spam, while still allowing modules and event-driven challenge structure.

The result is a fast, high-skill racing experience where “what you do” on the track matters as much as “what’s on the track.”
